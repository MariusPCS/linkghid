You don't need an expensive server to take your first steps in AI. You need an NVIDIA GPU with CUDA support, as much VRAM as your budget allows, and at least 32 GB of RAM. We put three affordable systems side by side, each covering a different scenario.

## What makes a workstation "entry-level" for AI

For learning and small projects, the part that matters most is the GPU: popular frameworks (PyTorch, TensorFlow) use CUDA, so an NVIDIA card is the safe choice, and the amount of VRAM determines which models you can run and train locally. The CPU and RAM help, but VRAM is the real ceiling.

## Professional vs. consumer

The professional cards (Quadro / RTX A-series) in the Dell and HP workstations are certified and very reliable, but at the same budget they often come with less VRAM than consumer GeForce cards. For getting started with AI on a limited budget, more VRAM usually matters more than certification — which is why the RTX 4070 Ti 12 GB system is the most versatile of the three.

## Verdict

Choose by priority: VRAM and good value → Hyperbuild UltimateN; certified reliability for professional work → Dell Precision 3240; workstation power in a compact form factor → HP Z2 G9 Tower.
