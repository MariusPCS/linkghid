When the first heatwave hits, a portable air conditioner becomes the most sought-after cooling option: plug it in, run the hose out of the window, and you get cold air in minutes — no fixed installation. The best unit, though, isn't the one with the most BTUs; it's the one that matches your room size and how often you'll use it.

## Types of cooling

### Portable air conditioner (monobloc)

The mobile, wheeled unit that vents hot air through a window hose.

**Pros**

- No fixed installation or approvals — move it from room to room
- A quick fix for renters or homes where you can't mount a split unit
- Many models also dehumidify, ventilate and come with a remote

**Cons**

- Louder than a split, because the whole mechanism is in the room
- Slightly lower efficiency, and the exhaust hose lets some heat back in
- Takes up floor space, and some models need condensate emptying

### Split air conditioner (fixed)

The wall-mounted unit with the compressor outside — the standard for permanent cooling.

**Pros**

- Best efficiency and cooling for large rooms
- Quiet indoors, since the noisy part is outside
- Ideal as a long-term, year-after-year solution

**Cons**

- Needs professional installation and a higher upfront cost
- Can't be moved, and may require approvals in apartment blocks

### Air cooler and fan

The cheap alternative for less scorching days.

**Pros**

- Low cost and low power consumption
- Easy to move, with no hose or installation

**Cons**

- Doesn't actually chill the air like a compressor unit
- Evaporative coolers raise humidity, which can feel unpleasant

## What to look for

- **BTU capacity by room size:** as a rough guide, ~5,000–7,000 BTU for small rooms (up to ~15 m²), ~9,000–12,000 BTU for medium bedrooms and living rooms.
- **Energy efficiency class:** a better class means lower bills under heavy summer use.
- **Noise level:** matters most in the bedroom — check the dB figure.
- **Hot-air exhaust:** check the hose and window kit; a good seal makes a real difference.
- **Useful features:** dehumidification, timer, night mode, remote or smart app control.
- **Maintenance:** filter cleaning and, on some models, emptying the condensate.

## Recommendations by scenario

- **Small room, office or RV:** a compact 5,000–7,000 BTU unit, such as the AlecoAir AC05 Travel or Equation EQ 7000 BTU.
- **Bedroom or medium living room:** around 12,000 BTU, such as the Beko BEP12H or AlecoAir AM12 SMART COOL.
- **Permanent solution, if you can install it:** a fixed split unit is still the most efficient long term.

## Efficiency and running cost

- Insulate the room: draw the curtains during the day and keep doors closed.
- Seal the exhaust hose at the window so heat doesn't creep back in.
- Use the timer and night mode to cut consumption.
- Clean the filter regularly — a clogged filter reduces cooling and raises power use.

## FAQ

**How many BTUs do I need?** It depends on room size and sun exposure. As a simple rule, 5,000–7,000 BTU for small rooms and 9,000–12,000 BTU for medium-to-large rooms.

**Portable or split?** For flexibility and no installation, portable wins. For maximum efficiency in a fixed room, a split is still better.

**How much does it cost to run?** It depends on the energy class and how often you use it; class A/A+ and running it on a timer noticeably lower the bill.
